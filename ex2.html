The solar system is a gravitationally bound system consisting of the Sun, eight planets, various moons, dwarf planets, and countless asteroids, comets, and other small bodies. The Sun, a star, is at the center, and the planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune) orbit around it.

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in our solar system. It is a rocky body like Earth and is one of four terrestrial planets in the Solar System. Mercury has an equatorial radius of 2,439.7 kilometres (1,516.0 mi). It is less than half the size of Earth, which is about 7,926 miles (12,756 km) in diameter. Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is known as Earth's "twin" or "sister" planet due to its similar size and structure. Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known astronomical object to harbor life. It is the fifth largest planet in our solar system, a rocky terrestrial planet with one moon. Earth is unique for its ocean planet status, with most of its water held in a global ocean covering 70.8% of its crust.
